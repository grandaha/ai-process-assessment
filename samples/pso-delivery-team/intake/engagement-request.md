# Engagement Request — Delivery Transformation AI Assessment

> **Sample intake document — fictional.** Feeds Phase 1 (Scoping). All names, numbers, and systems are invented. See `../README.md`.

---

**From:** Dana Okafor, Chief Delivery Officer
**To:** [Assessment team]
**Date:** 2026-05-04
**Re:** Where should we place our FY27 AI & automation bets in Delivery?

---

## The ask

I want a short, defensible list of the AI and automation opportunities inside our delivery operation that are actually worth funding next year — ranked, with enough of a business case that I can take one or two of them into the FY27 investment committee and not get torn apart.

I do **not** want a generic "here are 40 things AI could do" deck. I have seen those. I want to know: given how *we* actually run projects, where does an intervention pay for itself, and where is it a science experiment? I need to be able to defend the recommendation to Marcus (our CFO), who is allergic to automation pitches after the Project Swift mess two years ago.

## Why now

Our delivery margins are under real pressure. Utilization is sitting at 68% against a 75% target, and the gap is eating the year. At the same time, our consultants spend an embarrassing amount of their week on administrative work — status reports, time entry, chasing approvals — instead of billable client work. Exit interviews keep naming "admin burden" as a reason people leave. And finance is on me about DSO, which is creeping toward 60 days because our invoicing is slow and full of rework.

So there are three things colliding: margin, retention, and cash. If AI or automation can take a real bite out of the administrative drag on the delivery org, that helps all three. But I need to know *which* bite, and I need numbers, not enthusiasm.

## What I will do with the output

By the end of Q3 (September), I have to submit a Delivery Transformation portfolio for FY27 budget. I will personally decide which one or two initiatives go into that submission. Your assessment is the input to that decision. If the work is good, I take the top recommendation to the investment committee in October. If it is not, I fund nothing and we wait a year — I would rather fund nothing than fund another Swift.

So the decision this engagement enables is concrete: **which delivery-side AI/automation initiative(s), if any, do I put in front of the FY27 investment committee — and what is the case for each.**

## Rough scope

In scope — the delivery operation itself:
- How we staff and resource projects
- How we scope and write SOWs
- Project status reporting
- Time entry and approval
- Deliverable quality review
- Invoicing and billing reconciliation
- Project financial forecasting (EAC / margin tracking)

Out of scope, at least for this round:
- Sales and pipeline generation (that is owned by the growth org, different sponsor)
- HR / recruiting / L&D
- Anything that touches the *content* of client deliverables in a way that would put client-confidential material into an external tool without controls. I am open to opportunities there, but they have to come with a clear data-protection story or the committee will kill them on sight.

## What "good" looks like

- A ranked shortlist, not an inventory
- Each recommendation tied to a real baseline from our own operation (volume, cycle time, error rate, effort) — if you cannot baseline it, I do not want to see a savings number next to it
- A rough-order-of-magnitude business case for the top initiatives, honest about confidence
- A clear read on which ones are "do it inside current roles" versus "requires reorganizing how the team works"

## Constraints you should know

- **Budget:** I have roughly $1.5M of discretionary + committee-gated transformation money to work with for FY27. Anything bigger needs a separate capital case.
- **Timeline:** I need the recommendation in hand by **end of Q3 FY26 (September 2026)** to make the budget cycle.
- **Politics — read carefully:**
  - Marcus (CFO) chairs the investment committee and is openly skeptical of automation ROI after Project Swift overran and got shelved. He will want hard numbers and a credible sourcing story. Do not hand-wave the cost side.
  - Priya, who runs our Resource Management Office, is protective of how staffing decisions get made. If an opportunity looks like "the algorithm decides who staffs what," she will resist, and she has the relationships to slow it down. Frame anything in that area as augmentation, and be honest about it.
  - Our Quality Office is wary of anything that looks like AI grading client deliverables. There is a standards-and-reputation concern there that is legitimate, not just turf.
- **Access:** You will get good data out of our delivery systems. You will *not* get unrestricted access to client deliverable content — that is confidential, and some of it is under client NDAs.

## Who you will talk to

I will make myself available for the strategic framing. For the detail, the people who actually know how the work runs are Priya (resourcing), Tom (engagement management / SOWs), Sofia (project management — status and forecasting), Aisha (Quality Office), and Greg (billing / project finance). Marcus and a couple of practice leads can give you the upstream/downstream view.

Come back with something I can defend.

— Dana
